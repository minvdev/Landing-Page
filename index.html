Tenor Trombone

600

Standard in orchestras, jazz bands, and school ensembles.

Uses a telescoping slide to change pitch instead of valves.

Typically has a bore size between 0.500" and 0.547", balancing ease of play and tonal richness.

Some models include an extra tubing section with a trigger to extend the range.

Bass Trombone

900

Usually between 0.562" and 0.578", producing a deeper, richer sound.

Typically 9.5” to 10.5” in diameter for a fuller, more resonant tone.

Often features two rotary valves (F and D or Gb) for greater range flexibility.

Designed for playing in the bass register, requiring more air support.

Valve Trombone

1200

Similar to a trumpet, it has three or four valves instead of a slide.

Allows quicker technical passages compared to slide trombones.

Popular in mariachi and jazz due to its agility and bright tone.

Often shares bore and bell sizes with standard tenor trombones.
